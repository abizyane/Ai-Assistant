# Piscine

The Piscine is a 26-day intensive bootcamp that serves as the selection and onboarding process for 1337 Coding School. It is a rigorous introduction to programming and the school's unique learning methodology.

## Overview

The Piscine is mandatory for all new students and acts as both a selection mechanism and an intensive introduction to the school's culture and teaching methods. During these 26 days, students are immersed in daily coding challenges and collaborative learning.

## Daily Structure

Each day of the Piscine follows a consistent pattern:

- **Rush Exercises**: Daily coding challenges that must be completed within a set timeframe. These exercises introduce fundamental programming concepts and problem-solving techniques.
- **Peer Collaboration**: Students work together, share knowledge, and help each other understand concepts.
- **Evening Reviews**: Time for reflection and preparation for the next day's challenges.

## Programming Focus

The Piscine primarily focuses on the C programming language, providing students with a strong foundation in:

- Memory management and pointers
- Data structures and algorithms
- System-level programming concepts
- Problem-solving under time constraints

## Peer Evaluation (Correction)

A key component of the Piscine is the peer evaluation system:

- Students evaluate each other's work based on predefined criteria
- Evaluations are called "corrections" and follow a structured format
- The correction process teaches students to think critically about code quality and design
- Feedback from peers is essential for improvement

## Friday Exams

Every Friday during the Piscine, students take formal exams to assess their understanding of the week's material. These exams:

- Test both theoretical knowledge and practical coding skills
- Are conducted under controlled conditions
- Provide an objective measure of progress
- Help identify students who may need additional support

## BlackHole and Deadlines

The Piscine introduces the concept of the "BlackHole" — a strict deadline system:

- Each project and exercise has a specific deadline
- Missing a deadline results in the work being rejected and lost
- The BlackHole timer creates urgency and teaches time management
- This system continues throughout the student's time at 1337

## Bocal Staff

The Bocal (a term used at 1337 for staff members) provides support during the Piscine:

- They monitor student progress and well-being
- They facilitate the peer evaluation process
- They provide guidance on school policies and procedures
- They help resolve conflicts and technical issues

## Selection Outcome

Upon completion of the Piscine, students are evaluated for admission to the main curriculum. Selection is based on:

- Overall performance in exercises and exams
- Ability to work collaboratively
- Demonstration of problem-solving skills
- Commitment to the school's values and methodology
