# Curriculum

The 1337 Coding School curriculum is built on a project-based learning model that emphasizes peer collaboration and self-directed learning. Students progress through five distinct levels, each introducing increasingly complex programming concepts and real-world applications.

## Core Curriculum Structure

The curriculum spans approximately 21 major projects distributed across the five levels. Unlike traditional schools with lectures and instructors, 1337 employs a peer-learning methodology where students learn by doing, collaborating with classmates, and receiving feedback through peer evaluations.

## Programming Specializations

Students can choose from multiple specialization tracks after completing the core curriculum:

- **C/C++ Systems Programming**: Deep dive into low-level programming, memory management, and system-level applications.
- **Web Development**: Full-stack web technologies including frontend frameworks, backend services, and database design.
- **Systems Administration**: Infrastructure, DevOps, containerization, and cloud deployment.
- **Algorithms & Data Structures**: Advanced algorithmic problem-solving and optimization techniques.
- **Artificial Intelligence**: Machine learning, neural networks, and AI applications.

## Project-Based Learning

Each project is designed to teach specific competencies through hands-on implementation. Projects typically involve:

- Building real applications or tools
- Solving complex algorithmic challenges
- Implementing system-level features
- Collaborating with peers on larger codebases

## Self-Paced Progression

Students advance at their own pace through the curriculum. There are no fixed class schedules or cohorts. Instead, students work independently and in groups, with flexibility to focus on areas of interest while maintaining progress through required projects.

## Evaluation and Advancement

Progression through levels is determined by successful project completion and peer evaluation. Students must defend their work and demonstrate understanding of the concepts implemented. The evaluation process ensures that advancement is based on genuine competency rather than time spent.

## Continuous Learning

The curriculum emphasizes continuous improvement and learning from mistakes. Students can retry projects multiple times, and the feedback from peers and evaluators helps guide their development as programmers.
