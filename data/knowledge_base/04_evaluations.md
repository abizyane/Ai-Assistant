# Evaluations

The evaluation system at 1337 is a cornerstone of the peer-learning model. It emphasizes collaborative assessment, constructive feedback, and continuous improvement through structured peer review processes.

## Peer-Correction Process

The peer-correction system is the primary method of evaluation at 1337:

- Students evaluate each other's work rather than relying solely on instructor feedback
- This process teaches students to think critically about code quality, design, and best practices
- Corrections are conducted in a structured format with predefined evaluation grids
- Both the evaluator and the evaluated student learn from the process

## Defense Format

When a student's work is evaluated, they must defend their implementation:

- The student explains their approach and design decisions
- They answer questions from the evaluator about their code
- They demonstrate that their solution works correctly
- They discuss any challenges they faced and how they overcame them

## Evaluation Grid

Each project has a specific evaluation grid that outlines:

- Mandatory requirements that must be met
- Bonus features that can earn additional points
- Code quality criteria such as style, efficiency, and maintainability
- Specific technical competencies being assessed

The grid ensures consistency and fairness in evaluations across different evaluators.

## Retry Rules

Students have multiple opportunities to succeed:

- Each project can be attempted up to three times
- After each evaluation, students receive feedback on areas for improvement
- Retries allow students to refine their work and demonstrate mastery
- The final grade is based on the best attempt

## Exam Structure

Formal exams at 1337 follow a structured format:

- Exams are conducted under controlled conditions to ensure fairness
- Students are given specific problems to solve within a time limit
- Exams test both theoretical knowledge and practical coding skills
- Results are recorded and contribute to overall progress assessment

## Points, Levels, and XP

Student progress is tracked through a gamified system:

- **Points**: Awarded for completing projects and passing evaluations
- **Levels**: Students progress through levels as they accumulate points and complete required projects
- **XP (Experience Points)**: Additional rewards for exceptional work, helping peers, and demonstrating mastery

This system provides clear milestones and motivation for continuous improvement.

## BlackHole Timer

The BlackHole system enforces strict deadlines:

- Each project has a specific deadline for submission
- Missing the deadline results in automatic failure and loss of the project
- The timer resets for each new project
- This system teaches time management and accountability

## Feedback and Improvement

The evaluation process is designed to be constructive:

- Evaluators provide specific, actionable feedback
- Students learn from both successful and unsuccessful attempts
- The peer-review process builds a culture of continuous improvement
- Regular feedback helps students identify and address weaknesses
