# Admissions — 1337 Coding School

The 1337 admissions process is intentionally rigorous and aims to evaluate resilience, collaborative aptitude, and problem-solving. Prospective students normally apply online, submit minimal identity information, and register for the "Piscine" — a month-long, intensive hands-on coding bootcamp that tests foundational readiness. Selection criteria emphasize ability to learn quickly under pressure rather than prior credentials.

Typical stages:
- Online registration and basic eligibility.
- Small diagnostic exercises to confirm interest (short self-paced tasks).
- Piscine participation and peer assessment.
- Final selection interview or portfolio review.

Key points:
- Piscine is typically 4 weeks, immersive and collaborative.
- Candidates are expected to be present and participate in pair programming and team projects.
- Peer feedback is used heavily in early decisions.

Eligibility:
- Age: typically 18 to 30, but variations exist.
- No prior coding experience required.
- Motivation, perseverance, and team spirit emphasized over diplomas.
